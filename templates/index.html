      Help

      Build your fleet by photographing each car — AI instantly identifies the car type, reporting marks, road number, and color.

      • Click + Add Car via Photo to photograph or upload an image. AI fills in all fields automatically.
      • Click 🗄️ Select From Library to pick from previously uploaded photos.
      • Use ✨ Generate Stylized Illustration to create an artistic rendering of your car.
      • Click any car card to open its full details: waybills, movement history, location.
      • Edit or delete a car from its detail view. Deleting a car removes all associated waybills and movement history.
      • Cars can be added manually if you prefer not to use a photo.

      Waybills route cars between locations. Each car can have up to 4 sequential waybill slots — a full operating cycle.

      • Open a car's detail view and use the Waybills tab to add, edit, or reorder waybill slots.
      • Each waybill defines: origin, destination, industry, commodity, and whether the car travels loaded or empty.
      • Use Generate Waybills (Waybills tab) to automatically create waybills from your layout industries.
      • Use Auto-Assign to match unassigned waybills to available cars by type and routing logic.
      • Define commodity rules (e.g. grain → covered hopper) in Layout Setup to guide auto-assignment.
      • At session end, each car's active waybill slot advances automatically to the next slot.

      Define the geography of your model railroad: locations, industries, and commodity rules.

      • Locations have four types: Yard (staging hub), Industry (car destination), Staging (off-layout staging), Storage (parked cars awaiting assignment).
      • Industries are attached to locations and have a role: Consumer (receives loaded cars), Producer (ships loaded cars), or Transload (both).
      • Use ✨ AI Suggest next to any industry name to let AI fill in commodities, accepted car types, and role automatically.
      • Commodity → Car Type mappings tell the auto-assign system which car type to use for each commodity.
      • Use ✨ next to any commodity to get an AI suggestion for the correct car type.
      • Use ⚙ Seed Defaults to populate a starter set of commodity→car-type mappings.
      • Configure the Fast Clock start time and speed in the Fast Clock section at the bottom.

      Run a realistic ops session from plan to wrap-up. The Operations tab drives the session lifecycle.

      • Click 🚆 Plan Session to generate the session's switch list from current car positions and waybills.
      • The switch list is divided into three groups: Set out from staging (cars arriving at industries), Cars to spot (storage cars needing placement), and Pick up for staging (cars departing industries).
      • For each car, tap Done when the car has been moved to its destination, or CP (Car Placement) to temporarily park it at another location.
      • Click any car thumbnail to open its full detail view mid-session.
      • When all cars are worked, click ⬛ End Session. Waybill slots auto-advance and car locations update.
      • CP cars require you to assign a location before the session can end.
      • Use ✕ Cancel Session to abandon a session without saving any moves.
      • Session state persists across browser refresh — pick up where you left off.

      The fast clock simulates prototypical railroad time during a session. A 4× clock means 15 real minutes = 1 model hour.

      • Configure Start Time and Speed in Layout Setup → Fast Clock section.
      • Speed options: 1× (real time), 2×, 4×, 6×, or 12×.
      • The clock starts automatically when you plan a session and appears in the session header.
      • Click ⏸ to pause the clock; click ▶ to resume.
      • Clock state is stored on the server — refreshing the page resumes the clock from the correct model time.
      • The clock stops automatically when the session ends or is cancelled.

      WAYPOINT uses AI in several places to reduce manual data entry. All AI features require an API key configured in your .env file.

      • Car identification — Upload a photo of a car and AI identifies type, reporting marks, road number, and color.
      • Stylized illustration — The ✨ AI panel on the car add/edit form generates an artistic rendering using an image-generation model.
      • Industry suggest — The ✨ AI Suggest button in the industry form fills in commodities, car types, and role based on the industry name.
      • Commodity suggest — The ✨ button in the commodity mapping form suggests the correct car type for any commodity.
      • Supported providers: Gemini (default), Claude (Anthropic), GPT-4o (OpenAI), or a local Ollama model. Set VISION_PROVIDER in .env.
      • AI calls automatically retry on transient provider errors (rate limits, overloads) before failing.

      Export and restore your entire layout — locations, industries, cars, waybills, photos, and movement logs — in a single ZIP file.

      • Use Export Backup (bottom of any screen, or via the Layout Setup tab) to download a complete snapshot.
      • Use Import Backup to restore from a ZIP. This replaces all current data.
      • Backups are useful for sharing layouts with other operators or recovering from errors.
      • Use Purge Orphaned Photos to remove uploaded images no longer attached to any car.
      • Each car's detail view shows its last 5 movements with timestamps and notes.
